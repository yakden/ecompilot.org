## Overview

KSeF e-Invoices provides full compliance with Poland's National e-Invoice System (Krajowy System e-Faktur). Create structured electronic invoices, submit them directly to KSeF, handle GTU goods and services classification codes, and keep working even when you are offline. The complete workflow covers invoice creation through to acceptance or rejection, with download and preview at every stage.

## Key Features

- **Invoice creation with line items** — add products or services with quantity, unit price, VAT rate, and optional GTU codes per line.
- **KSeF submission** — submit invoices electronically to the national system directly from the app.
- **GTU code support** — select the correct goods and services classification (GTU-01 through GTU-13) as required by Polish tax law.
- **Buyer NIP validation** — verify the buyer's tax identification number (NIP) before issuing an invoice to prevent errors.
- **VAT rate selection** — choose from standard Polish VAT rates (23%, 8%, 5%, 0%) or exempt status per line item.
- **Payment method tracking** — record payment method (transfer, cash, card) and payment status on each invoice.
- **Status workflow** — invoices move through draft, submitted, accepted, and rejected states with clear status indicators.
- **Offline mode** — create and edit invoices without an internet connection; they sync and submit when connectivity returns.
- **Download and preview** — view invoice previews and download PDF or XML versions at any time.

## How It Works

1. Navigate to **KSeF e-Invoices** from the dashboard.
2. Click **Create Invoice** and fill in the buyer details. The system validates the NIP against the government registry.
3. Add line items — for each item, set the description, quantity, unit price, VAT rate, and GTU code if applicable.
4. Choose the payment method and set the payment due date.
5. Save the invoice as a draft to review, or submit it directly to KSeF.
6. Track the invoice status — once KSeF processes it, the status updates to accepted or rejected. If rejected, review the error details and resubmit.
7. Download the invoice as PDF for your records or share it with the buyer.
